About Me

Welcome! I'm Nahshon, a passionate and dynamic engineer dedicated to driving innovation at the intersection of Electrical Engineering, Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and sustainable development. I hold a B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering (First Class Honors) and a Master’s in Telecommunications Engineering from Dedan Kimathi University of Technology, Kenya. During my master’s program, I was supervised by Prof. Dr. Ciira Wa Maina of the Center for Data Science and Artificial Intelligence, where I developed a strong foundation in cutting-edge technologies and research.

Currently, I’m a Research Associate at the Ubiquitous Computing Group , led by Prof. Dr. Kristof Van Laerhoven at the University of Siegen , Germany. I specialize in LoRaWAN integration with Wearable Sensor Networks. My work bridges the gap between advanced communication protocols and everyday wearable devices, aiming to create solutions that are not only smart but also sustainable.

Pursuing a Ph.D., my goal is to achieve scholarly excellence in teaching and research, contributing significantly to the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) through innovative applications of AI and Data Science. I thrive in team-oriented environments, bringing a performance-driven mindset and a relentless enthusiasm for learning and embracing positive change.

Beyond the lab, I am actively involved in outdoor leadership and mentorship programs, where I inspire and motivate others to reach their full potential. Whether leading a team on a challenging hike or guiding a mentee through complex engineering problems, I believe in fostering growth and achieving dreams together.

Let’s connect and explore how we can collaborate to create impactful and sustainable technological solutions!
